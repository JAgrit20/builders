  1. Sponsor. Sponsor of the Fix-The-Internet Spring MVP Lab Challenge (the “Challenge”) is Mozilla Corporation, 331 E. Evelyn Avenue, Mountain View, CA, 94041.

Odds of winning the Challenge depend on the number and quality of eligible entries received during the Challenge Period. VOID WHERE PROHIBITED AND WHERE ANY REGISTRATION, BONDING OR LOCALIZATION REQUIRED.

    To enter and participate in the Challenge, you must fully complete the Challenge submission form on the Challenge page available at https://mozilla2.forms.fm/fix-the-internet-spring-lab/forms/7758/responses/new and submit your entry as provided on the Challenge page and in these Official Rules. You may enter as a Team (as defined below) or on a provisional basis as an individual. Teams may have no more than four (4) members. By submitting your entry, you are agreeing to these Official Rules on behalf of yourself and all Team members, and by participating all Team members agree to the Official Rules as a participating entrant. A Team will collectively also be deemed an entrant. Your entry submission information must include (i) your name and email address, (ii) your idea name, (iii) a description of your idea, (iv) a description of your work plan, (v) all other information listed as required in the contest application form, and, if applicable, the names of all Team members. Your initial entry must also contain a short written presentation of your idea. One entry per individual or Team. An individual may only participate on one team. Any attempt by any participant to obtain more than the stated number of entries by using multiple/different email or addresses, accounts, identities, or any other methods will void that participant's entries and that participant may be disqualified.

The “authorized account holder” is the natural person assigned an email address by an Internet access provider, online service provider or other organization responsible for assigning email addresses for the domain associated with the submitted address. A potential winner may be required to show proof of being the authorized account holder. In the event of an ongoing dispute as to any entrant, Sponsor has the right to make determination as to entrant(s) in its sole discretion.

    For a group of individuals to enter the contest (a “Team”), the Team must agree to and designate one person as the agent of the Team to submit the entry and to accept the prize on behalf of the Team. Each team must have a software engineer (someone able to write software code) to be eligible to enter the Challenge. Each individual on a Team must agree to these Official Rules. You may not make changes to your Team once registered to participate. By submitting an entry, the submitter represents that all Team members have read and agreed to these Official Rules.

    If an individual enters the contest without a team and is accepted, it will be on a provisional basis. The individual will have two (2) weeks to form an eligible Team. If the individual successfully forms a Team, the team will be subject to these Official Rules. If the individual does not sucessfully form an eligible Team, the individual will be disqualified from the Challenge.

    All entries must meet the following criteria:

    For the First Phase, entries must include:
    • All team member names, email addresses, and descriptions,
    • The idea name,
    • A short, written description (e.g., write-up, PowerPoint slides) that presents the entry’s idea(s) or “pitch”
    • A description of your for work plan,
    • A description of the challenges you anticipate,
    • The team’s remote working condition, and
    • Area category for your idea.
    For the Second Phase:
    • Entries must include a shippable “Minimum Viable Product” with the core features proposed in the Team’s First Phase application, reasonably bug free, and safe to download. Any change to the proposed core features must be discussed and approved by the Expert Judges.

By submitting likeness of any individual, you represent that you have received permission from such person to include their likeness.

    • Entries must be in English.

  5. Finalists and Winners. In the First Phase, there will be up to twenty (20) winning Teams selected. There will be no ranking.

    In the Second Phase, there will be three (3) winners selected. They will be ranked 1st, 2nd, and 3rd.

    Winners will be announced on or about July 1, 2020. The overall “best” entries will be selected as winners.

    All entries will be reviewed and evaluated by a panel selected by the Sponsor (“Expert Judges”). Judging by the Expert Judges will occur in two phases. In reviewing the entries, all Judges will be looking for degree of alignment with one or more, but not limited to the following criteria::

    • Relevance to Topics
      • Creatively and effectively addresses the issues raised in one or more of the Challenge Area Categories: Collaboration & Society, Decentralized Web, Messaging & Social Networking, Misinformation & Content, Surveillance Capitalism, Artificial Intelligence. These categories are described more fully on the Challenge website, available at: https://builders.mozilla.community/springlab/index.html
    • Quality of Projects
      • Scope - Teams propose and execute against a scope of work that can be implemented over an 8 week period.
      • User Experience - Attention is given to create user interfaces and user experiences that are simple and easy for users to grasp and engage with.
      • Differentiation - Reflects an awareness of existing work in this space and proposes a solution differentiated from existing products and services.
    • Promise
      • Proposed project demonstrates a clearly understood problem and that potential to have usefulness and impact for to significant populations of users in a given market segment or many market segments.
    • “Completeness”
      • Final project is “complete” based on proposals and proposal refinements made between the participants and their mentors during the program.
      • Complete projects should be functional, relatively bug-free, and safe to use or download on a device.

    For First Phrase entries, the Expert Judges will also consider the following criteria:

    • Skillsets. Team skillsets and previous experience are relevant to the proposed concept including having one or more team members with the ability to design and program software. Ability to participate and collaborate with team members and the program remotely over virtual communication channels such as video conferencing software and/or messaging.

    For Second Phrase entries, the Expert Judges will also consider the following criteria:

    • Product Quality. The quality, efficiency, security, privacy, and effectiveness of the product.
    • Alignment with the Mozilla Mission and Manifesto. You can find the Mozilla Mission here: https://www.mozilla.org/mission/
